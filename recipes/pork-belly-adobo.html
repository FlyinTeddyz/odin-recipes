Pork Belly Adobo with Garlic Fried Rice

Back

A Dish made by Disneyland I recreated

This is a dish made by Disneyland during their Festival of Holidays I recreated. Contains pork belly, garlic fried rice, green onions, and chicharrón.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork belly
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 1/2 tbs brown sugar
  • black pepper
  • cooked white rice
  • 2 heads of garlic
  • avocado oil
  • 6 cloves roasted garlic
  • 3 tbs garlic oil
  • green onion
  • chicharrón

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 250 degrees
  2. Peel garlic cloves and add to loaf pan
  3. Cover garlic cloves with enough avocado oil to submerge the garlic
  4. Cook garlic in preheated oven for 2 hours. Transfer to jar when cooled
  5. Cut pork belly into desired shapes
  6. Season pork belly with black pepper
  7. Make sauce by combining soy sauce, vinegar, water, and brown sugar. Stir until sugar dissolves
  8. Fry pork belly with roasted garlic oil in dutch oven until brown
  9. Reduce heat to medium, medium-low and add the sauce and cover for 1 1/2 hours or until pork belly is tender
  10. While pork belly is almost done, combine cooked (preferably day old) rice with 4 tbs of roasted garlic oil
  11. Fry rice until hot and no clumps of rice are present
  12. Mince 6 cloves of roasted garlic and add to fried rice
  13. Continue to for another 5-10 minutes
  14. Chop green onions
  15. To plate, add rice first, pork belly adobo on top, garnish with green onions and crumbled chicharrón
